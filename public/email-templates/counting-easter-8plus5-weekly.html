Bidding focus: doubles

This week we lean into doubles — when to make them, and how to respond when partner doubles for takeout. It is one of the most common areas for partnership misunderstandings, so a little structured practice goes a long way.

Below are two quick “table moments” in the same spirit as the site problems — no answer buttons here; they are just to set the scene.

Example 1 — judgment

Auction: 1♦  ?

You (South)

♠K Q 10 7 3
♥A Q 4 3
♦5
♣Q 10 3

What would you bid here — double or 1♠?

On the site we walk through simple, repeatable ideas so this kind of decision — which comes up all the time — feels familiar rather than fuzzy.

Example 2 — responding to a double

Auction: 1♣  X  P  ?

You (South)

♠K Q 10 7 4
♥3
♦K J 3
♣10 9 8 2

What would you bid here?

Same theme — partner has doubled, you have values but not quite enough for game. The practice problems unpack how to describe that hand without guessing.

Those snapshots mirror the kind of questions the bidding trainer answers step by step — clear auctions and short explanations after you try your answer. If doubles and responses have ever felt murky, this week is a good time to chip away at them in order.
